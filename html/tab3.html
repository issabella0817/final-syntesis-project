General Relativity

What is it?


General relativity is a theory of the gravitational field and general reference systems, published by Albert Einstein in 1915 and 1916. What was it about? The theory of general relativity proposes that the very geometry of space-time is affected by the presence of matter, resulting in a relativistic theory of the gravitational field. In fact, the theory of general relativity predicts that space-time will not be flat in the presence of matter and that the curvature of space-time will be perceived as a gravitational field. Einstein's basic insight was to postulate that at any certain point one cannot experimentally distinguish between a uniformly accelerated body and a uniform gravitational field. The general theory of relativity also made it possible to reformulate the field of cosmology.

gravity

At the beginning of the 20th century, Albert Einstein developed his general theory of relativity, in which he described gravity as a deformation of space caused by the presence of massive objects, similar to what happens when a heavy ball deforms a rubber strip. . This deformation "indicates" the smaller objects as they should move through space, so that they enter into orbit or fall on the larger astronomical object. This gave rise to a very different way of visualizing space. In the past, it was believed that space contained a fluid known as "ether", but when no one was able to prove the existence of this substance, humanity began to think that space was simply empty.
